Types of Autism

Autistic Disorder (Classic Autism)

This is the most severe form of autism, involving significant difficulties in communication, social interaction, and repetitive behaviors. Children may have delayed speech, struggle with eye contact, and exhibit strong sensitivity to sensory inputs.

Asperger’s Syndrome

Children with Asperger’s have normal or advanced language skills and high intelligence but struggle with social interactions. They may have intense interests in specific topics, difficulty understanding social cues, and prefer routine


Pervasive Developmental Disorder-Not Otherwise Specified (PDD-NOS)

This is a milder form of autism where children show some but not all classic autism symptoms. They may have mild social and communication challenges but can still function relatively well in daily life.

Childhood Disintegrative Disorder (CDD)

A rare and severe condition where children develop normally for a few years and then experience sudden loss of language, motor, and social skills. This regression usually occurs between ages 2-4 and is more severe than other autism spectrum disorders.
